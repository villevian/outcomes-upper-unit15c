🔴 Forced Output Tasks

🎤 Task 1: The Food Scandal Press Conference

Student = CEO of a company that made "coriander ice cream". Teacher = aggressive journalist.
🎙 Teacher (Journalist)
1. "People say your new burger is a crime against humanity. Do you admit that it's healthy only on paper?"
💼 Student must use
deny + -ing → e.g. "I absolutely deny making unhealthy food!"
🎙 Teacher
2. "Our investigation shows tons of your food goes uneaten and ends up in the bin. Why do you allow this criminal waste?"
💼 Student must use
refuse to + do AND insist on + -ing → e.g. "We refuse to accept these claims and insist on reducing waste."
🎙 Teacher
3. "You pioneered plant-based burgers, but critics say they taste like cardboard. How do you respond?"
💼 Student must use
urge someone to + do AND encourage someone to + do → e.g. "I urge critics to try it first and encourage everyone to keep an open mind."
🎙 Teacher
4. "Will you resign over this scandal?"
💼 Student must use
threaten to + do OR agree to + do → e.g. "I threaten to sue anyone who spreads lies" / "I agree to launch an internal review."
🎙 Teacher
5. "Your own staff confessed to cutting corners with ingredients."
💼 Student must use
warn someone to + do AND advise someone to + do → e.g. "I warn you not to trust rumors and advise the public to wait for official results."

🎬 Task 2: The Netflix "Blind Pitch"

Student pitches a movie idea using Unit 1 vocabulary.
📝 Framework for student
1. Background: "It's an adaptation of… / It has an astonishing cast."
2. Plot: "The plot revolves around… / It tackles the issue of…"
3. Hook: "It's totally gripping and uplifting."
4. Honest flaw: "Some scenes are over-the-top, but the acting saves it."
🎙 Teacher provocations
1. "Sounds like another remake that relies on predictable clichés. Why should I invest?"
2. "Is it going to be a box-office hit, or just too weird for the mainstream?"
3. "Your last project flopped. Convince me this one's different."
💼 Must use reporting verbs
suggest + -ing, consider + -ing, imagine + -ing
e.g. "I suggest watching the trailer first" / "Just imagine seeing this on the big screen!" / "Consider investing — you won't regret it."

💰 Task 3: The "Angry Client" Confrontation

Student = bank manager. Teacher = furious client whose card got eaten by the cashpoint.
🎙 Teacher (Client)
1. "The cashpoint ate my card, and now I'm strapped for cash! This is a total rip-off!"
💼 Must use
apologize for + -ing AND passive promise → e.g. "I sincerely apologize for causing this inconvenience. Your card will be returned within 24 hours."
🎙 Teacher
2. "You charged me commission for a transfer that has been delayed for 3 days!"
💼 Must use
confess to + -ing AND intend to + do → e.g. "I confess to overlooking this issue. We intend to refund the commission immediately."
🎙 Teacher
3. "I'm closing my account unless you fix this NOW!"
💼 Must use
worry about + -ing AND remind someone to + do → e.g. "I understand you worry about losing money. Let me remind you to check your updated statement tomorrow."

✍️ Task 4: Live Writing (Synthesis)

Student writes a LinkedIn/Twitter post about the scandal. Teacher upgrades vocabulary in real time.

✓

✓

✓
"Critics accused the company of hiding the truth and warned consumers not to trust the brand."

📋 Pattern Reference Card

Quick-look cheat sheet for delayed correction
V + to do
agree to do · refuse to do · threaten to do · intend to do
V + sb + to do
urge sb to do · advise sb to do · encourage sb to do · remind sb to do · warn sb (not) to do
V + -ing
deny doing · suggest doing · consider doing · imagine doing
V + prep + -ing
apologize for doing · confess to doing · insist on doing · worry about doing
V + that clause
confess · deny · imagine · insist · suggest · threaten · warn · worry

⚠️ Common Traps

✗ deny to do → ✓ deny doing
✗ suggest to try → ✓ suggest trying
✗ insist to make → ✓ insist on making
✗ urged to rethink → ✓ urged THEM to rethink
✗ apologize to sell → ✓ apologize for selling
✗ confess doing → ✓ confess to doing
